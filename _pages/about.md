I am currently a **Schmidt AI in Science Postdoctoral Fellow at Cornell University**, where I develop trustworthy and interpretable AI systems for scientific and engineering applications. I received my **Ph.D. in Control Science and Engineering from Zhejiang University in 2023**, with research focused on the **reliability, robustness, and explainability of data-driven models** for industrial systems. Before joining Cornell, I was a **Research Fellow at Nanyang Technological University**, where I worked on robustness and failure analysis for multi-agent learning systems in industrial settings.

My research lies broadly in **trustworthy machine learning**, with an emphasis on building AI systems that are reliable, explainable, and useful in real-world environments. I am particularly interested in the intersection of **computer vision, generative modeling, anomaly diagnosis, industrial AI, and AI for science**.

My current and recent research interests include:

- **AI for Plant Science:** Developing vision and generative AI methods for plant phenotyping, pathology analysis, and structural reconstruction, with the goal of enabling interpretable and scalable plant intelligence for agriculture and climate resilience.
- **Computer Vision and 3D Reconstruction:** Building end-to-end pipelines for perception tasks such as semantic segmentation, monocular depth estimation, and single-view 3D reconstruction for scientific and engineering applications.
- **Explainable AI:** Designing feature attribution and interpretation methods for deep models, especially for high-stakes tasks such as anomaly diagnosis, fault localization, and scientific decision support.
- **Robust and Reliable Machine Learning:** Studying adversarial robustness, failure modes, verification, and reliable model behavior under perturbations and domain shifts.
- **Anomaly Detection and Fault Diagnosis:** Developing data-driven methods for fault detection, classification, and localization in industrial processes, especially under data scarcity, imbalance, and shifting operating conditions.
- **Optimization for Machine Learning:** Applying optimization techniques, including mathematical programming and robust learning strategies, to model design, verification, and performance improvement.

My work has been published in venues including **IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI)**, **IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems**, **IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics**, and **Computers and Electronics in Agriculture**.
